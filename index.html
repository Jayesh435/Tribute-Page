Baba Jumdevji - A Spiritual Beacon

About Baba Jumdevji

Mahantyagi Baba Jumdevji was a well-known spiritual leader and social reformer who dedicated his life to social upliftment and empowerment. He was born on April 3, 1921, and from a young age, he showed signs of spiritual inclination and leadership.

Life and Teachings

Baba Jumdevji's teachings focused on self-discipline, service to humanity, and the importance of living a virtuous life. He was also a vocal advocate against social evils like alcohol consumption and child marriages.

His Legacy

His vision led to the creation of a dairy cooperative and a small bank in 1976 to support the local community. His efforts have left a lasting impact on the lives of many and continue to inspire people to this day.

Early Life

Born as Juman Thubrikar in a humble weaver's family, Baba Jumdevji spent his early childhood in the neighborhood of Golibar Chowk in Nagpur. His spiritual journey began at a young age, marked by unusual events and a deep sense of connection to the divine.

Enlightenment and Service

At the age of 21, Baba Jumdevji attained enlightenment and made vows that shaped his path of service. He became a beacon of hope, providing spiritual guidance and practical solutions to the problems faced by his followers

Community Service

Understanding the needs of his followers, many of whom were poor farmers, Baba Jumdevji established a dairy cooperative and a small bank in 1976, empowering them to earn a steady livelihood and become self-reliant

Recognition

In honor of his contributions, a postal stamp was released on October 1, 2013, by the then Vice President of India, Dr. Hamid Ansari, commemorating the life and work of Baba Jumdevji.

In loving memory of Baba Jumdevji (1921-1996)
